You can choose whether to use an **attack** or a **skill** on your enemies.   
   
Attacks and Skills   
You can attack with whatever weapon you have currently equipped on you. While it doesn't do the most damage, it will not deplete your HP or SP.   
   
Attacks and Skills   
Launch an attack by selecting the target and pressing .   
   
Attacks and Skills   
On the other hand, using a skill will deplete your HP or SP. To use one, press , then select the skill you want to use and the target you want to use it on.   
   
---   
   
Weaknesses and 1 More   
Each skill and attack has an **affinity**, and enemies will have weaknesses towards certain affinities.   
   
Weaknesses and 1 More   
Attacking an enemy with its weakness will deal more damage than usual. It will also knock down the enemy.   
   
Weaknesses and 1 More   
When an enemy is down, you'll be able to attack **1 More** time.   
   
Weaknesses and 1 More   
If you use this extra turn to knock down a different enemy, you can attack 1 More time again. As long as you keep knocking the enemies down, you'll be able to attack continuously.

All-Out Attack   
When you've knocked down every enemy, you'll get the chance to execute an **All-Out Attack** that will deal significant damage to all enemies.   
   
All-Out Attack   
Once an All-Out Attack is possible, there will be a prompt asking whether or not you want to initiate it. Press  to cancel and  to perform an All-Out Attack. Choose wisely depending on the situation.   
   
All-Out Attack   
If you are afflicted with a status ailment, or if you are the only one mobile at the time, then an All-Out Attack cannot be executed.   
   
All-Out Attack   
If any party member is afflicted with a status ailment at the time of an All-Out Attack, they will not be able to join in. This will reduce the amount of damage dealt to enemies.

Equipment is listed in four categories: Weapons, Armor, Footwear, and Accessories.   
   
Equipment Types   
Weapons primarily influence attack damage, Armor reduces the damage taken by the wearer, Footwear affects the wearer's evasiveness, and Accessories grant the wearer various special effects.   
   
Equipment Types   
Sometimes, equipment other than Accessories will also grant special effects. Try out different combinations to optimize and specialize your loadout.

Aohige Pharmacy   
Aohige Pharmacy sells all kinds of recovery items. You'll want to check their stock on a regular basis, and keep an eye on their seasonal product rotation.

Sale Dates   
Inventory goes on sale on Monday at Tatsumi East Police Station and on Saturday at Aohige Pharmacy. Remember to take advantage of these discounts.

Mayoido Antiques   
You can now travel to Mayoido Antiques to conduct an Item Fusion or Item Exchange.   
   
Mayoido Antiques   
Item Fusions involve expending one weapon part and a handful of required materials to create a unique piece of equipment.   
   
Mayoido Antiques   
In the Item Exchange, the proprietor will take any gemstones off your hands in exchange for items.   
   
Mayoido Antiques   
Materials that are used in Item Fusions can often be found in Tartarus, so you'll be able to collect them as you explore.   
   
---   
   
Elizabeth's Requests   
You can now speak to Elizabeth about accepting and reporting requests.   
   
Elizabeth's Requests   
Requests can involve anything from hunting for items to fusing specific Personas. When you complete a request, you will be rewarded.   
   
Elizabeth's Requests   
You might see an influx of requests during specific times of the year. Additionally, there are requests that only become available after completing certain previous requests.   
   
Elizabeth's Requests   
Some requests have deadlines. If you miss the deadline, the request will **expire**, and you will no longer be able to complete it.   
   
---   
   
Outings with Elizabeth   
As you complete more requests, Elizabeth will start asking you to escort her to different places from time to time.   
   
Outings with Elizabeth   
You can accompany her on these outings any time during daylight hours.   
   
Outings with Elizabeth   
Going out with Elizabeth will take no time at all, so you don't need to worry about planning your day around her.

Combat Characteristics   
Combat Characteristics are passive effects that will automatically activate during battle. Your party members each have unique Combat Characteristics which become available as you deepen your bonds.   
   
Combat Characteristics   
Select Status from the  menu for further details on a party member's Combat Characteristics under Theurgy.

Missing Persons Reports   
Due to the influence of Shadows, civilians will sometimes get lost in Tartarus and become **missing persons**.   
   
Missing Persons Reports   
Missing persons will be somewhere in Tartarus, and **must be rescued within a set time limit**.   
   
Missing Persons Reports   
In the event someone goes missing, details will be posted on **the bulletin board in front of the police station**, so keep an eye peeled.

Accessing Downloadable Content   
Purchased DLC can be redeemed by checking the closet in your room.

Treasure Chests   
Treasure chests in Tartarus contain **items**, **money**, and other loot that will make exploration easier.   
   
Treasure Chests   
There are **many types** of treasure chests, each with different contents inside. You especially want to keep an eye out for special items that can only be obtained from treasure chests.

Using Items and Skills   
If you get injured while exploring Tartarus, you can use items and skills to restore HP or SP.   
   
Using Items and Skills   
Open the  menu, select **ITEM**, and choose a recovery item, such as  or . Then choose a party member.   
   
Using Items and Skills   
Skills work in a similar way. Start by opening the menu, select **SKILL**, and pick a recovery skill, such as **Dia**. Then choose a party member.   
   
Using Items and Skills   
It's best to prioritize Minato's HP so that he doesn't faint while exploring.

Types of Attacks and Encounters   
You can swing your sword by pressing  while exploring to slash an enemy. Doing so will start combat.   
   
Types of Attacks and Encounters   
Striking an enemy from behind will trigger a **Chance Encounter**, where **you have the advantage** at the start of combat. If the enemy attacks you first, a **Pinch Encounter** will occur, **placing you at a disadvantage**.   
   
Types of Attacks and Encounters   
Get into the habit of initiating Chance Encounters to gain the upper hand for an easy victory.   
   
Types of Attacks and Encounters   
You can still swing your sword even when there are no enemies present, so you'll be able to practice and test your sword's range.   
   
---   
   
One-Way Teleporters   
Occasionally, you will encounter **one-way teleporters** in Tartarus that can be used to return to the entrance.   
   
One-Way Teleporters   
Keep in mind that, since these teleporters are only one-way, **you cannot return to the same floor where it was used**.   
   
---   
   
Returning from Tartarus   
After returning from Tartarus, **the Dark Hour will end**. This will start the next day. Use this time wisely to recuperate for your next expedition.   
   
Returning from Tartarus   
**The structure of Tartarus will have changed** by your next expedition. **Treasure chests you have already found can be rediscovered** as well, so returning could be a good idea if you find yourself stuck.   
   
---   
   
Saving in Tartarus   
While exploring Tartarus, you can save at **locations where you can change your party**. However, you cannot save on the floor you're currently exploring.   
   
Saving in Tartarus   
You can save from the menu or by pressing .   
   
---   
   
Dashing   
You can **dash** by pressing and holding  while moving. You'll cover ground more quickly, making your exploration of Tartarus more efficient.   
   
Dashing   
However, **enemies will spot you more easily when dashing**, so be careful.   
   
---   
   
Auto-Recover   
You can press  during an exploration to automatically recover HP. A party member's recovery skill will be used to **instantly restore HP for all party members**.   
   
Auto-Recover   
Using a recovery skill to auto-recover HP will also cost SP, so if you run out of SP, you will not be able to auto-recover. Be sure to monitor your SP whenever you use it.   
   
---   
   
Locked Treasure Chests   
Special, locked treasure chests can be unlocked with Twilight Fragments. The amount you need will vary from chest to chest, so you'll want to collect as many as you can.   
   
---   
   
Changing the Party   
You can decide who to take into combat at **locations where you can change party members**, such as the entrance of Tartarus.   
   
Changing the Party   
You can change party members by talking to your teammates or by opening the menu with  and selecting **STATUS**.   
   
---   
   
Clocks   
The clock at the entrance can be used to fully restore HP and SP **for your entire team**.   
   
Clocks   
You can also find clocks on the floors you're currently exploring. They will also provide the same effect as the clock at the entrance.   
   
Clocks   
Activating a clock requires **seven Twilight Fragments**, so you should make a habit of collecting them for emergencies.   
   
---   
   
Minimap and Movement Shortcuts   
While exploring, you can press  to open the **Minimap**, which shows your current location as well as the path you traveled.   
   
Minimap and Movement Shortcuts   
It also displays information on **enemy and treasure chest locations**, so don't hesitate to check it every now and then.   
   
Minimap and Movement Shortcuts   
**After finding the stairs**, you can use the shortcut to move to the next floor **from any spot**. It could be worth your while to scour the current floor before moving on to the next.   
   
---   
   
Teleporters   
Not all teleporters are one-way. You will sometimes encounter another type of **teleporter**.   
   
Teleporters   
This teleporter works like an elevator. It allows you to **travel between floors** with teleporters or return to the entrance. Use them to explore Tartarus more efficiently.   
   
---   
   
Rare Shadows   
While exploring Tartarus, you may encounter elusive **rare Shadows**. Defeating them will net you **hefty rewards**.   
   
Rare Shadows   
Rare Shadows will attempt to flee if they spot you. They'll keep running until **they successfully escape**. You'll want to ambush them from behind and strike quickly.   
   
---   
   
Formidable Shadows   
While exploring Tartarus, you may encounter the rare **Formidable Shadow**.   
   
Formidable Shadows   
Defeating them will yield greater rewards than usual, but **brace yourself for a trying battle** and take care not to become the hunted.   
   
---   
   
Changing Costumes   
Press  to open the menu, then select **EQUIP** to change costumes. They're **purely cosmetic**, but they can offer a change of pace while exploring.   
   
---   
   
The Reaper   
While inside Tartarus, the **Reaper** may appear if you **stay on the same floor for too long**.   
   
The Reaper   
The Reaper is a terrifying foe. You'll want to avoid combat and hurry to the next floor as quickly as possible.   
   
---   
   
Scouting   
After combat, you may have the opportunity to **scout**. If you decide to scout the area, one of your teammates will temporarily leave your party to look for a path to the next floor.   
   
Scouting   
The ally scouting ahead will also collect items and hand over anything they've collected upon rejoining the party on the next floor. Note that you will be down a party member if you choose to scout.   
   
---   
   
Enemies on the Run   
When Minato's level is **significantly higher** than the enemies' on the floor, **they will begin to flee** from him. This gives you the luxury of collecting treasure chests unhindered and exploring more efficiently.   
   
---   
   
Border Floors   
As you ascend Tartarus, you will reach **border floors** that prevent you from progressing any further. As there is no way to dispel these barriers, you'll have to **wait for the right time**.   
   
---   
   
Missing Persons   
Sometimes you will encounter **missing persons** who have wandered into Tartarus.   
   
Missing Persons   
Missing persons cannot be rescued **after a full moon**, so it's best to save anyone you find as soon as possible.   
   
Missing Persons   
You can **receive a reward at the police station** the day after a successful rescue, so don't forget to stop by.   
   
Missing Persons   
You can **ask Elizabeth** where the missing persons are located. Seek her advice regularly to rescue those who are lost in Tartarus.   
   
---   
   
Monad Doors   
Sometimes **a Monad door** will appear in Tartarus. Defeating the special enemy inside will trigger a special Shuffle Time, where **Major Arcana cards appear more frequently**.   
   
Monad Doors   
Defeating the special enemy will also reveal **the layout of the current floor and the floor directly above it** once you exit through the door.   
   
Monad Doors   
The Monad door is an unstable contraption. When one appears on a floor, the door will lose power and become inaccessible if you move to another floor, even if you return using a teleporter on the same day.   
   
---   
   
Monad Passage   
The Monad Passage is a type of Monad door leading to **the depths of Tartarus**. There is **one per section of a block**. Each Monad Passage opens in response to a **Major Arcana card**.   
   
Monad Passage   
A **Formidable Shadow** resides in the innermost area, and beyond it lies a treasure chest holding a **valuable item**.   
   
Monad Passage   
The entrance to the Monad Passage will remain permanently on the same floor in the same location, **even after the turn of a new day**. If you feel outmatched, consider revisiting after training some.   
   
---   
   
Fuuka's Skills   
Fuuka's skills support the party not only during combat, but also during exploration.   
   
Fuuka's Skills   
She learns unique utility skills such as **Jamming**, which renders you undetectable to foes.   
   
Fuuka's Skills   
Press  during exploration to activate Fuuka's abilities. Like your other teammates' skills, this costs SP, so use items to recover if you're running low.   
   
---   
   
Fuuka's Skills: Jamming   
Jamming is a support skill that **makes you undetectable to enemies**. Use it when you're running low on HP or when you want to pick up treasure chests without being detected.   
   
Fuuka's Skills: Jamming   
Jamming is **ineffective against certain enemies**, and will be disabled once you **enter combat or change floors**.   
   
---   
   
Fuuka's Skills: Sylphid Aura   
Sylphid Aura is a support skill that **automatically raises the Defense and Accuracy/Evasion** of the **whole party** at the beginning of combat.   
   
Fuuka's Skills: Sylphid Aura   
The effect duration **lasts between floors**, but will be canceled **after entering battle**. It is best utilized before decisive battles and key encounters.   
   
---   
   
Fuuka's Skills: Shock Noise   
Shock Noise is a support skill that **distresses enemies on the current floor**. Though it can be **ineffective against certain enemies**, it should help make exploration more efficient.   
   
---   
   
Fuuka's Skills: Escape Route   
Escape Route is a support skill that allows you to instantly **return to the entrance** when activated. Use it when you're low on SP if you want to regroup.   
   
---   
   
Fuuka's Skills: Tartarus Search   
Tartarus Search is a support skill that analyzes and **reveals the full map** of the floor you're currently on. As it reveals the stairs, it will unlock the option to **shortcut to them**.

Ambush   
You can now **Ambush** enemies while exploring. When Ambushing an enemy, you'll trigger Chance Encounters **regardless of the direction an enemy is facing**.   
   
Ambush   
Press and hold  to dash for a set distance to enter the **assault state**. By pressing  while in this state, you will **Ambush** the enemy.   
   
Ambush   
Triggering an encounter from behind will slightly **increase Minato's Theurgy gauge** and may **Distress** the enemy at the beginning of battle.

Shadows Afflicted with Status Ailments   
Some enemies roaming around Tartarus may already be afflicted with **status ailments**. They will move differently depending on the ailment, which will **take effect at the start of combat**.   
   
---   
   
Status Ailment: Rage   
The **Rage** ailment causes one to lose their sense of reason. Enraged enemies will aggressively target you, **even from a distance**.   
   
Status Ailment: Rage   
They will **charge upon spotting you**, but **struggle to redirect the force of their trajectory**, so keep your calm, sidestep, and wait for your opening to attack.   
   
---   
   
Status Ailment: Confuse   
Enemies who are **Confused** will roam around unsteadily, as though drunk.   
   
Status Ailment: Confuse   
**They move slowly, and are prone to missing their attacks**, making them prime targets to pick off with Chance Encounters.   
   
---   
   
Status Ailment: Fear   
Enemies afflicted with **Fear** will **attempt to flee if they see you**.   
   
Status Ailment: Fear   
These enemies will **freeze in terror** when you make eye contact with them, but **attack you in a frenzied panic** if you turn your back. Beware.

Entrance to the Great Clock   
If you've used a  in Tartarus, an entrance to a special dimension with a **Great Clock** may appear.   
   
Entrance to the Great Clock   
The Great Clock grants a large experience boost to a teammate of your choosing. Although you can only choose allies at a lower level than Minato, the greater the level difference, the bigger the bonus.   
   
Entrance to the Great Clock   
Remember, the entrance will only appear after you've used a **Twilight Fragment**, so don't be shy about using them.   
   
Entrance to the Great Clock   
However, entrances to the Great Clock will **vanish** if you move to another floor, so be sure to use them when they appear.

Dark Zone   
While exploring, your periphery may occasionally become shrouded in a darkness known as the **Dark Zone**.   
   
Dark Zone   
With your vision obscured, you'll need to use your map to find your way around. Fortunately, **enemies can't detect you either**, so you can take your time looking for the stairs to the next floor.   
   
Dark Zone   
**Breakable objects** are also guaranteed to contain a , so don't be deterred by the darkness and take your time exploring.   
   
---   
   
Greedy Shadows   
You may encounter **Greedy Shadows** while exploring. These Shadows amass items by looting treasure chests, so expect **greater rewards** for defeating them than rare Shadows.   
   
Greedy Shadows   
Greedy Shadows are always scampering helter-skelter, so catching them is **a matter of luck**. If you lose sight of one at a juncture, **track them to the best of your ability**.   
   
Greedy Shadows   
Even if one slips away, they will sometimes **drop items**. The more correct guesses you make on **which way they go at junctures**, the more items they'll drop.   
   
---   
   
Breakable Objects   
Within Tartarus, you will find **breakable objects** comprised of Shadow remains. Destroying them will sometimes **yield items**, so get wrecking.

Rewinding to a Previous Point   
When you save your game, it also saves **a little bit of your past**. You can go back to that time by opening the menu with , going to SYSTEM, and selecting REWIND.   
   
Rewinding to a Previous Point   
When you select a REWIND slot, you will be returned to a point where you could roam freely shortly before you saved your game.   
   
Rewinding to a Previous Point   
Note that the REWIND function **does not save your new starting point automatically**. If you use this function, make sure you continue to save your game as necessary.

Uploading Save Data to the Network   
By saving your game in the NETWORK SLOT, you can upload save data online instead of saving it to the main console storage.   
   
Uploading Save Data to the Network   
You will need an account for PlayStation™Network to utilize the NETWORK SLOT.   
   
Uploading Save Data to the Network   
Game data saved using this method can be transferred between PlayStation®4 and PlayStation®5 consoles.   
   
Uploading Save Data to the Network   
However, trophies earned during gameplay on one console **will not transfer** to another console that you transfer your save data to.   
   
Uploading Save Data to the Network   
Keep in mind, certain trophies **cannot be earned unless you start a new game from the beginning or with clear data**, if you have not already earned them.   
   
Uploading Save Data to the Network   
Also, trophies based on numerical values, such as completing an action a certain number of times or attaining a certain amount of something, will be **reset to zero upon transferring to a different console**.
